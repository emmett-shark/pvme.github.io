    ‎ ‎Common Mistakes Made in Combat and How to Fix Them

    The following list notes some of the common mistakes made by players in PvM. It is likely that if you're working on upping your DPM, you will need to improve in one or many of these areas. The first step is recognizing what mistakes you're making, then work on the solution. Don't be discouraged if you don't get it right away, some of these mistakes are harder to fix than others. Remember, everything takes practice.

    Cancelling Channelled Abilities Late or Early

    Channeled Abilities

    ⬥ Concentrated Blast : 2hit (3t/1.8s)

    ⬥ Snipe : (4t/2.4s)

    ⬥ Fury : 2hit (3t/1.8s)

    ⬥ Asphyxiate : 4hit (7t/4.2s)

    ⬥ Rapid fire : 8 hit (8t/4.8s)

    ⬥ Assault : 4hit (7t/4.2s)

    ⬥ Destroy : 4hit (7t/4.2s)

    ⬥ Greater Flurry : 2 or 3 hit(3t/1.8s or 5t/3.0s)

    Note: The listed cancel timings are standard, depending on overall rotation the cancel time you use may differ.

    Why it’s Important

    Depending on what ability you cancelled late, you will lose anywhere from 1 to 3 ticks every channeled ability you don’t cancel properly.

    As an example, in a 5 minute gem you will use up to 12 Asphyx's , assuming you don't cancel them, it will add up to 21.6 seconds that you’re standing idle for. That’s enough time for 10 extra abilities. Canceling too early is also suboptimal as you lose out on the damage from the channeled ability.

    How to Fix

    #Evil Lucario's video guide on correct canceling timing:

    https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=unCtj7IosVA

    Note: The buff bar timers shown are outdated in the video, use !cancelling in #unknown-channel instead to get up-to-date timers.

    Threshold Prioritisation During Your Ultimate

    Why it’s Important

    With Magic and Ranged , the expected number of thresholds with an Adrenaline Potion is 6. with Melee using up to 3. These numbers may vary depending on your adrenaline gained with other sources.

    How to Fix

    Take a look in #dpm-advice-mage #dpm-advice-range #dpm-advice-melee to see the optimal threshold order.

    Threshold Prioritisation Outside Your Ultimate

    Why it’s Important

    Some thresholds have different damage values when used inside a Sunshine , Death's Swiftness , and Berserk , it is thus necessary to understand their differences for proper priorization.

    How to Fix

    Take a look in #dpm-advice-mage #dpm-advice-range #dpm-advice-melee to see the optimal threshold order.

    Ability Prioritisation During Your Ultimate

    Why it’s Important

    As silly as it may sound, pressing stronger buttons will greatly increase your DPM moreso than any other gear upgrade.

    How to Fix

    Familiarize yourself with which abilities you should prioritise at any given time: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1OetEUz4xU5sg7pqmJBkkafmZIye8qoPOXprUPGhin14/edit?usp=sharing

    Lossless Auto Attack at the Start of Your Ultimate

    Cause

    Not releasing the auto attack, or releasing a dual wield auto attack.

    Why it’s Important

    It’s a lossless attack (no lost ticks).

    How to Fix

    Use a dual-wield ability before activating your ultimate, and immediately after activating your ultimate switch to a 2h and click on the target to obtain a free auto attack before the GCD is over.

    Clicking the target is only necessary if you clicked your Planted Feet/2h switch from your inventory. If you keybound everything, disregard clicking the target; the auto will come with no extra input needed.

    Utilizing Flanking in Your Rotation

    Why it’s Important

    Your basic Flanking is a 260% ability and is better than nearly any other basic ability in terms of damage.

    How to Fix

    Practice. Make an effort to use it as often as possible.

    Ability Prioritisation in Your Rotation

    Why it's Important

    Prioritizing strong abilities over weaker abilities.

    How to Fix

    For Revolution, take a look at the official RuneScape Wiki and set up an optimized revolution bar.

    For full manual, take a look at this spreadsheet to understand which abilities are stronger. https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1OetEUz4xU5sg7pqmJBkkafmZIye8qoPOXprUPGhin14/edit?usp=sharing

    Managing Cooldowns of Good Abilities

    Why it’s Important

    Your good abilities often come with a long cooldown, using them first all at once will leave you with weaker abilities for a large period of time

    How to Fix

    By using filler abilities such as Sonic Wave , Concentrated Blast , Dazing Shot , Needle Strike , Fury , Cleave , and Decimate (abilities that have decent damage with a short cooldown) in-between the strong abilities, you can craft a rotation where you will never run into a scenario where you have to resort to weaker abilities.

    Losing Ticks

    Cause

    Not inputting the next ability fast enough, having trouble determining what is the next ability, or clicking and being slow on weapon switches

    Why it’s Important

    Same rationale as canceling channels. This adds up overtime to lost abilities.

    How to Fix

    Practice, try and think 2 abilities ahead and plan out your rotation.

    Changing Weapons Mid-Channel Ability

    Why it’s Important

    Changing your weapon mid-Conc will cancel it. Thus this requires precise timing to make sure you’re still getting 2 hits from your conc blast. Further, changing your main-hand mid-Conc will lose you the crit bonus from the ability.

    How to Fix

    Practice the timing.
